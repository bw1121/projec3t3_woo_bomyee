Bojack’s Career Timeline

1980s

  • Amateur Standup Comic

    Performed comedy routines at hole-in-the-wall comedy clubs with his friend Herb, trying to become big stars.

  • Horsin’ Around

    Hit TV comedy series starring BoJack about a horse raising three kids. This put Bojack’s name on the map and he soon rose to fame. Most people recognize him from this role.

1990s

  • Restaurant Elephante

    Owner of a restaurant in Hollywoo that serves variety of food and drinks. He often frequents the restaurant and hosts parties for himself and his friends.

  • The BoJack Horseman Show

    Originally titled “Mitch’s Life” was critically panned and canceled. It is still unknown what exactly happened to this series.

2010s

  • One Trick Pony

    Biography about the life of BoJack written by Diane Ngyuen. The book portrayed the struggles of the past and present life of BoJack and revealed a lot of personal detail that made audiences relate on a personal level to BoJack.

  • Secretariat

    BoJack played the lead character in a movie based on the life of Secretariat, a 1973 “Triple Crown winner” and BoJack’s childhood hero.

  • Career Summary

    BoJack’s career started when he moved to Los Angeles in the mid-80s and began performing as a comedian in small comedy clubs and bars.

    He grew to fame when his longtime friend landed a show called ‘Horsin’ Around’ and casted him as the star of the show. The sitcom centered around a young bachelor horse (BoJack) raising three orphans.

    After the show ended, BoJack entered a period where he did not take any roles in the entertainment industry. However, in the 2000s, he started working with ghost writer Diane to write a biography about his life.

    The book was a hit and it allowed him to be casted in his long-awaited dream in playing Secretariat. BoJack hopes to become someone greater than just “the horse from ‘Horsin’ Around’”. He longs to become an award-winning actor playing deep, serious roles in the future.
